Google, in collaboration with RECF, PwC US, India Community Center and Team 315 Paradigm is hosting two in-person Girl Powered Robotics Workshops at the ICC (India Community Center) in Milpitas, CA.

The workshops are for girls in Grades 3 to 5 on July 27-28, 2024, and Grades 6 to 12, 2024, on August 3-4, 2024. The workshops are geared towards girls and offers the students an opportunity to design and build a robot from scratch and experience first hand the joys of solving real world engineering challenges through friendly competition.

Girl Powered is an initiative to redefine the face of STEM by creating a more inclusive environment for young girls within the robotics community. The Girl Powered Workshop is an in-person event hosted to inform young girls about STEM and robotics opportunity.

FAQ

Last updated: Jan 10, 2024

  • Which workshop is right for my student?

    • VIQ is open to students in elementary school (Grades 3 to 6), and VRC for middle school and high school students (Grades 6 to 12). If your team has mostly students in grades 3 to 6 in 2024-25 then VIQ will best suit them.

  • The student has participated in IQ previously but not in VRC. Can they be part of VRC this year?

    • Yes, students can be part of VRC.

  • Students who participated in VRC previously would like to attend it this year as well.

    • The purpose of the workshop is to introduce robotics to students who haven’t had exposure to robotics before. We ask that students only attend the VRC workshop once to allow others the experience.

  • What is the minimum requirement to attend this workshop?

    • This event is for all girls, and no prior robotics or programming experience is needed. It’s a great way to introduce robotics to those who may be curious, and we have sessions planned for a wide range of experience levels and interests.

  • I am unable to find a mentor.

    • The purpose of a mentor is to oversee the students and help the girls in time management during the session. Any adult can be the team mentor as no technical experience is required.

  • Where do I mention the names of students I would like to be grouped with on the same team?

    • In the express interest form and the RobotEvents registration form, there is a field where you can mention the names of the students you would like to be grouped with. We will try our best to match you to your preferences. If you don’t know the names at the time of application, you can later email girlpowered.robotics.goog@gmail.com as long as it’s before June 15, 2024.

  • Can parents/guardians enter the venue?

    • The entry to the venue is restricted to the registered team mentor and the students who registered for the workshop.

  • How to mention dietary restrictions?

    • Due to the size and scale of the event we will provide only Vegetarian and Non-vegetarian (Regular Chicken/Turkey) food options. If your student has dietary restrictions we recommend that they bring their own food.

For any further queries please reach out to girlpowered.robotics.goog@gmail.com
